Research overview

I am generally interested in fluid dynamical problems in rotating stratified systems, motivated by geophysical and astrophysical systems (mostly focusing on the ocean). I employ a variety of computational and mathematical tools to tackle related problems. Areas I have worked on and/or are particularly interested in are:

  • Wave/eddy-mean flow interactions (global ocean circulation, eddy parameterisation)
  • General Circulation Model developments
  • Inverse methods for oceanography
  • Fluid instabilities (in particular shear instabilities)
  • Tracer mixing/stirring

Numerical models play a very large role in my research though I am relatively agnostic about which models to use. I go from bespoke small-scale models (largely in MATLAB and Python) to global circulation models (NEMO and MITgcm). Recent projects (archived projects)

GEMOETRIC (Geometry and Energetics of Ocean Mesoscale Eddies and Their Rectified Impact on Climate)
primary collaborators: David Marshall (Oxford), James Maddison (Edinburgh), Scott Bachman (NCAR)
papers: MMMM18 JPO, MMMB17 OM, BMMM17 OM

(David has a concise summary of it on his webpage.) In its present form GEOMETRIC is, from a practical point of view, a reworking of the well-known Gent-McWilliams parameterisation for mesoscale eddies arising from baroclinic instability. How the baroclinic eddies are represented in a numerical model can lead to large changes in the global ocean circulation through changes to the Southern Ocean residual overturning circulation.

Spilhaus projection of the ocean
schematic of SO RMOC taken from Burke et al. (2015), GRL

I have implemented the GEOMETRIC scheme in various forms into a hierarchy of numerical models, ranging from custom models of the Southern Ocean to global ocean circulation models in NEMO. In particular, we demonstrate the use of GEOMETRIC leads to improvements of global circulation characteristics to changed in surface wind forcing, notably the eddy saturation and the eddy compensation phenomenon. This in turn have implications for things like energy, carbon and oxygen cycles for changes to tracer ventilation.
